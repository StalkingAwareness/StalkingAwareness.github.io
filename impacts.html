Impacts on Victim-Survivors

Stalking is a crime that can not only extend beyond years, but lead to physical, sexual, and even fatal violence. The impacts are just as extensive, from impacting mental, physical, and social health, to impacting victims financially, and their job or school.

Effects on mental health
  • Confusion and self-doubt, questioning if they are over-reacting
  • Fear that they or others will be harmed
  • Insecurity and the inability to trust others
  • Guilt, embarrassment, self-blame
Effects on social life
  • Insecurity and inability to trust others
  • Avoidance of usual activities
  • Others withdrawing from the victim because they don't believe the victim, or consequence of victimization
  • Victim moving to a new area, changing phone number, name or even appearance
Effects on finances
  • Relocation
Prevent from seeking help
  • Not understanding what is happning is stalking or illegal
  • Fear that others will think they're over-reacting or that they are to blame for encouraging it in some way
  • Fear of retaliation from the stalker, their work
  • Financial limitations
