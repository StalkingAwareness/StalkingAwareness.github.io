Stalking is defined as

A pattern of behavior directed at a specific person that would cause a reasonable person to feel fear for their safety or the safety of others; or suffer substantial emotional distress.

Who Is Stalked

Over the past 12 months it is estimated that 13.5 million people in the United States have been stalked in one form or another, only a third of those cases were ever reported, and less than two percent of stalking cases reported resulted in a conviction. One in three women and one in six men are stalked at some point in their life About half of victims report being stalked before turning 25 Nearly 1 in 4 were stalked before 18 People 18-24 experience the highest rate of stalking victimization (among adults) 40% of victim-survivors are stalked by a current/former intimate partner. 42% by an acquaintance
