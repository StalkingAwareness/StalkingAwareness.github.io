Stalking is defined as

"A pattern of behavior directed at a specific person that would cause a reasonable person to feel fear for their safety or the safety of others; or suffer substantial emotional distress."

Who Is Impacted?

  • Over the past 12 months, it is estimated that 13.5 million people in the United States have been stalked in one form or another.
  • Only a third of those cases were ever reported.
  • Less than two percent of stalking cases reported resulted in a conviction.
  • 1 in 3 women and 1 in 6 men are stalked at some point in their life.
  • About half of victims report being stalked before turning 25.
  • Nearly 1 in 4 people were stalked before 18.
  • 18-24 year olds experience the highest rate of stalking victimization (among adults).
  • 40% of victim-survivors are stalked by a current/former intimate partner.
  • 42% by an acquaintance.
